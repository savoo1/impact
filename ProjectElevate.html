Project Elevate

The Company is a start-up social enterprise investing in high-achieving, low income students, providing them with the resources to attend four year colleges, graduate on time, and successfully enter the job market.

Impact profile

Key Terms

Financials

Program Related Investment (PRI) in Tanche I Note, which will fund college loans to students from low and moderate income families.

Senior unsecured notes to the Community Fund with downside protection of 12.5% first loss capital.

2.5% fixed rate plus variable payment on 50/50 split of the net yield of the portfolio between the Company and investors, 5 year maturity.

Current portfolio size of $425k, average loan size $2.5k - $15k, rates from 5.15-9.49% variable and fixed, 4% origination fee.

Since founding in 2015, the Company has raised $6mm of equity in its seed round which included family office and impact venture capital funds, and $2.5mm of venture debt from an institutional investor.

Impact

Increases access to higher education and the probability for economically viable, self-sufficient careers.

According to a study by the Education Trust, the lowest income students with the highest scores on eighth grade standardized tests are less likely to go to selective colleges than the highest income students with the lowest test scores. More than 20% of those high-achieving, low-income students do not go to college at all.

Tuition has more than doubled since 1970, while federal Pell Grant has dropped from covering 2/3 of college costs to 1/4th.

90% of the student loan market comes from federal sources. But these funds are not enough to complete four years of a selective college. Private financing solutions and Fintechs have stepped into fill this gap. The Company's borrowers area unable to obtain funding form other private sources (eg. banks), which represent $110bn outstanding but require prime credit or a co-signer.

In order to ensure that students have the highest likelihood for graduation and conversion to an economically viable career path, the Company works with a network of partners including federal programs, charter schools, post-secondary institutions and non-profit organization that specialize in college readiness, retention and graduation.
